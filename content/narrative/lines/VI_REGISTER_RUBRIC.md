# Vietnamese Register Rubric

Status: ready for external native-speaker review.
Applies to: `content/narrative/lines/vi.json`, `apps/web/messages/vi.json`, Vietnamese footer/legal/accessibility copy, and the Lumi tagline.

## Voice

CyberSkill Vietnamese copy should feel casual, warm, and precise. It should sound like a Vietnamese creative-technical studio talking directly to a capable visitor, not like a government notice, travel brochure, or machine-translated SaaS page.

## Register

- Use `bạn` for the visitor and `chúng tôi` for CyberSkill.
- Keep UI strings short, direct, and warm.
- Keep Lumi narration slightly lyrical, but still conversational.
- Prefer clear Vietnamese intent over literal English structure.
- Avoid slang that would age quickly or feel region-specific.

## Dialect

- Use standard written Vietnamese.
- Avoid strong Northern, Central, or Southern colloquial markers unless a line explicitly needs local Saigon color.
- Keep diacritics NFC-normalized and UTF-8.

## Narrative Lines

Narrative may be a little poetic when Lumi speaks. The sentence should still be easy to read aloud, fit captions, and preserve the scene beat.

Reference tagline:

`Lumi — vì ánh sáng biến nguyện ước thành sự thật`

## UI Strings

UI strings should be plain and task-oriented.

- Prefer `Gửi` over `Vui lòng gửi`.
- Prefer `Đổi ngôn ngữ` over formal phrasing.
- Prefer `Bỏ qua câu chuyện` over literal or awkward variants.
- Keep button labels compact enough for mobile.

## Reviewer Instructions

For every flagged string, record:

- Original.
- Suggested replacement.
- Decision: accepted or rejected.
- Rationale.
- Whether the issue is register, dialect, clarity, diacritics, cultural fit, or UI length.

If a reviewer recommends a more formal version, the founder may reject it when it conflicts with the brand voice above. Record that decision explicitly.

## Re-Run Trigger

Run this review again for any major Vietnamese content drop: new case studies, CTA forms, legal/accessibility copy, hiring copy, or new Lumi narrative lines.
