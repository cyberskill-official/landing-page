## Cultural Narrative

CyberSkill is a Vietnamese software studio founded in Ho Chi Minh City. The site is designed as a cinematic product story because the company’s work sits between imagination and implementation: a client arrives with a wish, and the team turns it into working software.

The visual anchor is Lumi, a small golden genie with a casual nón lá. The hat is not used as a tourism symbol or ceremonial costume. It is treated as a quiet everyday marker: Vietnamese, warm, practical, and close to the street-level creative energy of Saigon. The story moves from the first idea, through prototyping and craft, into team trust, Vietnam-to-global delivery, and finally a three-track call to action.

The Vietnamese tagline is intentionally revealed through interaction rather than placed as a billboard. English visitors can discover it once per session; Vietnamese visitors can reveal it naturally. That small interaction keeps the Vietnamese identity present without making it decorative.

The site also treats performance and accessibility as part of the design work: a `/lite` route exists for reduced-motion or no-3D users, captions mirror the narrative, and analytics are proxied without client-side tracking SDKs.
